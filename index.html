Intake

Engaging into a company transformation is a demanding and irrevocable process once started. A good understanding of the value creation, critical drivers for change and impact on the organization is key for a successful implementation. The Intake report provides this clarity by:

  • defining areas for improvement,
  • mapping priorities,
  • indicating timelines,
  • inventory of (potential) risks,
  • indicating Transformation project cost and Transformation related costs

Therefore the Intake report is mandatory before starting any Transformation Project. The Intake report cost is a one-off non committing fee, after receiving the Intake report the client is free to decide to enter into the next phase, kick-starting the Transformation Project, or not. FTR needs – at most - one week on-site assessment to report back on transformation changes needed to create the envisioned future organisation. Once agreed to the Transformation Plan the next step is creating a KPI driven staged Transformation Plan focusing on Management Assessment.

Management assessment

Present company status is a direct result of decisions taken by management anticipating on market- and customers dynamics, economy and linked to company strategy. As company future state and results are a concern, it makes sense to assess the competence and capabilities of senior management, middle management and to see if there is alignment of Board of Directors, Sr. Management, Head of Departments, Works Council, Unions, on the envisioned way forward.

Risk Mitigation

Based on the Management Assessments recommended way forward, FTR will list the risks related to the various scenarios and advice on mitigating actions The mitigation plan is part of the Roadmap.

Roadmap

The FTR Roadmap is a comprehensive, solution focused mapping of all required changes to create and sustain Value Creation of the new organization and includes:

  • Project Plan, highlighting KPI driven actions and clear milestones for timelines, deliverables and costs driving the transformation
  • HR Support dealing with redundancies, (dismissal process), preparing RfA (Request for Advice) and Works Council and Union consultations
  • Risk Mitigation Plan
  • Performance Management Program to ensure sustainability of the future organization
  • Communication Plan to create awareness, sense of urgency, create engagement and understanding
  • Sanity Check Program to secure agility of the organization

Cost Transparency

FTR is very clear in its communication, approach and costs. Our many years of expertise learned us to evaluate, create and translate a Transformation Plan into fixed costs allowing our clients to budget for change.

Depending on the scope of the Transformation Plan we offer various options to best fit our client’s requirements:

Fixed Intake fee

Engaging into a Transformation process is irrevocable therefor a good understanding of impact, steps and stages and costs are mandatory for a success implementation.

  • The Intake report provides this clearly and understanding prior to committing to the Transformation Plan.
  • The Intake will be done at a pre-agreed fixed rate.

Project Plan staged invoicing

  • The Project Plan will clearly indicate process and progress.
  • With a fixed fee per stage based on completion of pre-agreed, defined next steps and/or deliverables

All-in day rate with success based incentive

  • If the Transformation Plan is strongly dependency phased (e.g Works Council or Union related processes) we can also agree on a all-in Day-fee.
  • This fee is independent of the number FTR experts working on the Transformation, remote or on-site

All-in fixed Transformation Plan fee

FTR uniquely offers for mid- to large sized Transformation Projects an all-in fixed fee with a success based incentive for on time and within budget delivery
It allows for clear budgetting, full transparency on costs important for share- and stakeholders.
